Frequently Asked Questions

To prevent outdoor pipes from freezing, insulate them with foam pipe insulation or heat tape. Ensure outdoor faucets are turned off and drained before the first freeze. If possible, shut off the water supply to outdoor pipes and open faucets to allow any remaining water to expand without causing damage.

If you notice a leak in your outdoor plumbing, start by turning off the water supply to that section. Then, inspect the area to identify the source of the leak. Temporary fixes like plumbing tape can be used for small leaks, but it’s advisable to contact a professional plumber for a permanent solution.

To increase water pressure, ensure the hose bib is fully open and check for clogs or leaks in the hose or piping. Cleaning or replacing the aerator may also help. If pressure remains low, consider checking the main water valve and pipe size or consult with a plumber to inspect the system for any blockages or issues.

For cold climates, use pipes made of materials that can withstand freezing temperatures, such as PEX or copper. PEX is particularly popular due to its flexibility and resistance to bursting under freezing conditions. Ensure any exposed pipes are properly insulated.

Regularly inspect your outdoor plumbing for leaks or damage, especially in hoses, hose bibs, and sprinkler systems. Clean any debris from vents and drains to prevent clogs. Check for corrosion or wear on pipes and fittings, and repair or replace as needed.
