Frequently Asked Questions

It is recommended to perform routine plumbing maintenance at least once a year to ensure all components are functioning properly and to catch any potential issues early.

Basic DIY maintenance includes regularly checking for leaks, inspecting all visible pipes, ensuring all drains are clear, and testing shut-off valves.

Prevent clogs by using drain screens to keep out debris, avoiding pouring grease or coffee grounds down the sink, and regularly cleaning drains with baking soda and vinegar.

Signs include persistent leaks, slow drainage, frequent clogs, reduced water pressure, and any unusual noises in the plumbing system.

Knowing the location of the main water shut-off valve is crucial in case of a major leak or plumbing emergency, allowing you to quickly prevent water damage.
