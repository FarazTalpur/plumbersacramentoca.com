Frequently Asked Questions

First, ensure your safety by turning off the electricity to the affected area. Then, if possible, stop the source of the flooding such as a broken pipe. Contact a professional for assistance and begin removing excess water using pumps or wet/dry vacuums.

To prevent mold, dry the area as quickly as possible. Use fans, dehumidifiers, and ventilation to remove moisture. Remove and dispose of any porous materials like carpet and drywall that are soaked. Cleaning with disinfectants may also help to eliminate any mold spores.

You can call a professional water damage restoration company. They have the expertise and equipment to efficiently extract water, dry the space, and advise on repairing any damage.

Whether a flooded basement is covered depends on the cause of the flood and your insurance policy. Most standard homeowners insurance does not cover flood damage from natural causes unless you have specific flood insurance. However, if the flood is due to a burst pipe or similar issue, it might be covered.

Common causes include heavy rain, poor drainage around the foundation, sump pump failures, sewer backups, broken or leaking pipes, and nearby flooding or groundwater saturation.
