Frequently Asked Questions

A sewer camera inspection is a process where a high-resolution waterproof camera is inserted into sewer lines to visually inspect the condition and identify any blockages, leaks, or damage.

Sewer camera inspections help to accurately diagnose problems within your pipes without excavation. This can save time and money by targeting specific issues such as blockages, cracks, or tree root intrusions.

It's recommended to have a sewer camera inspection every 1 to 2 years for preventive maintenance. However, if you encounter recurring plumbing issues, it may be necessary to have an inspection more frequently.

While sewer camera inspections are highly effective for identifying many issues like blockages and structural damage, they may not detect every problem. For example, some minor leaks or issues that are hidden by build-up could be missed.

A typical sewer camera inspection usually takes about 1 to 2 hours, but this can vary depending on the length of the pipes and the nature of any discovered issues.
