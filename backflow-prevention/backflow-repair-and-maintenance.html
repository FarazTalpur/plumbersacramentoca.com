Frequently Asked Questions

Backflow is the reverse flow of contaminated or unclean water into the clean water supply, often due to pressure changes in the system. Preventing backflow is crucial to ensure the safety and purity of drinking water and to comply with health and safety regulations.

Backflow prevention devices should be inspected and tested at least annually by a certified technician to ensure they are functioning correctly and to maintain compliance with local health and safety regulations.

Common signs that a backflow device may need repair include water leaks, reduced water pressure, unusual noises from the device, or visible rust or corrosion. If any of these signs are present, it is essential to have the device checked by a professional.

There are several types of backflow prevention devices, including reduced pressure zone (RPZ) valves, double-check valves, pressure vacuum breakers (PVB), and atmospheric vacuum breakers (AVB). The choice of device depends on the specific needs and regulations of the water system.

While regular visual inspections can be done by homeowners, any maintenance, testing, or repairs to backflow prevention devices should be performed by a licensed and certified professional to ensure safety and regulatory compliance.
