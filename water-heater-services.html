Frequently Asked Questions

It is recommended to service your water heater annually to ensure it operates efficiently and to extend its lifespan.

Common signs include a lack of hot water, unusual noises, leaks, rusty water, or a significant drop in water temperature.

The average lifespan of a traditional tank water heater is about 8-12 years, while tankless water heaters can last up to 20 years with proper maintenance.

If your water heater is more than 10 years old, frequently needs repairs, or your energy bills are increasing, it may be more cost-effective to replace it.
