Frequently Asked Questions

To install a toilet, you typically need a wrench, a screwdriver, a hacksaw, plumber's tape, a level, a tape measure, and possibly a putty knife for removing the old wax ring.

Signs that your toilet may need repair or replacement include constant running, frequent clogs, leaks, and cracks in the porcelain. If repairs are frequent and costly, it might be more economical to replace the toilet.

A wax ring provides a waterproof seal between the toilet base and the drain pipe. It doesn't require regular replacement, but should be replaced any time the toilet is removed for servicing or repair to maintain an airtight seal.

While toilets can be installed on a variety of floor surfaces, it's important the floor is level and sturdy. Installing a toilet on an uneven surface might cause leaks or instability and may require shimming.

A constantly running toilet may be due to a faulty flapper, a float that is too high or sticking, or a fill valve issue. Start by checking the flapper for wear and ensuring the float is set correctly. Replace any faulty parts if necessary.
